When You Need a Date-of-Death Appraisal in Elk Grove

A date-of-death appraisal determines the fair market value of real property as of the date a property owner passed away. If you're handling an estate that includes property in Elk Grove, you may need this appraisal for handling estate tax disputes with the IRS, establishing the stepped-up cost basis for inherited property, filing IRS Form 706 (Estate Tax Return), or settling an estate after a loved one passes away.

Our desktop appraisal service provides a USPAP-compliant valuation prepared by a California Certified Residential Appraiser — accepted by the IRS, probate courts, and estate attorneys statewide.
